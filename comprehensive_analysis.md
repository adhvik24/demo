### Content Summary

This PDF contains **Chapter 1** from an NCERT English literature textbook titled **"I Sell My Dreams"** by **Gabriel Garcia Marquez** (1927-2014).

#### Story Synopsis

The story is a masterpiece of magical realism by Nobel Prize winner Gabriel Garcia Marquez. It revolves around:

1. **Main Character:** Frau Frieda - a Colombian woman living in Vienna who makes a living by "selling her dreams"
2. **Setting:** Multiple locations including Vienna (Austria), Barcelona (Spain), Havana (Cuba), and Oporto (Portugal)
3. **Time Period:** Spans approximately 34 years, from post-WWII Vienna to the 1980s

#### Key Plot Points:

1. **Opening Scene:** A dramatic incident at the Havana Riviera Hotel where a huge wave embeds a car into the hotel wall, killing a woman wearing a distinctive snake ring with emerald eyes

2. **Flashback to Vienna:** The narrator meets Frau Frieda in Vienna 34 years earlier, where she works for a wealthy Austrian family by interpreting their dreams to predict their daily fortunes

3. **Frau Frieda's Gift:** She has prophetic abilities through dreams, which she discovered as a child when she correctly predicted her brother's death (though not in the way her mother interpreted)

4. **Warning to the Narrator:** Frau Frieda dreams about the narrator and warns him to leave Vienna immediately and not return for five years - which he does

5. **Reunion in Barcelona:** Years later, they meet again in Barcelona with poet Pablo Neruda, where mutual dream experiences occur

6. **Final Revelation:** The woman who died in Havana is revealed to be Frau Frieda, who had been working for the Portuguese ambassador

### Literary Significance

This story exemplifies Gabriel Garcia Marquez's signature style of **magical realism**, where:
- Extraordinary events (prophetic dreams) are presented as ordinary occurrences
- Reality and fantasy blend seamlessly
- The narrative maintains ambiguity about whether Frau Frieda's powers are real or a clever survival strategy
- The circular structure connects the opening tragedy with the narrator's past
